Prok Sinigang

Classic Filipino Pork Sinigang

Sinigang is a classic Filipino sour soup dish. You can use pork, beef, fish or shrimp as the main protein, and we will be using pork in this recipe. The chicken version of this recipe is called Sinampalukang Manok. This is usually eaten with white rice, and is perfect when the weather is cold

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg pork belly - cut into pieces
  • 4 tomatoes - chopped
  • 2 onions - sliced
  • 5 pieces finger chilis
  • 1 pack string beans
  • 1 bunch kangkong or 1 pack spinach
  • 2 pieces taro
  • 3 tbps fish sauce/li>
  • 1 pack Knorr Sinigang Mix

Steps:

  1. Heat the vegetable/canola oil in a large pot, and saute the onions until they are translucent.
  2. Add the pork belly and cook until it turns golden brown.
  3. Add the tomatoes and taro. Pour the water into the pot and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat to medium-low when it boils, then cover it and simmer until the pork is tender and cooked through, approximately 30 minutes.
  4. Put the Sinigang Mix in, then stir. Add the chili and string beans, and simmer covered for 10 minutes.
  5. Put the spinach and simmer covered again for 3 minutes.
  6. Serve hot with rice, and enjoy!
