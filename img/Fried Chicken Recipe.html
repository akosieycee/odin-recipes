Filipino Fried Chicken

Fried Chicken - Filipino Version

Filipinos love Fried Chicken! All the restaurants and fastfoods in the Philippines serve fried chicken. I usually marinate my fried chicken in fish sauce and calamansi/lemon to ensure that the meat has flavour and is not bland. We then coat it in a flour and cornstarch mixture with spices, and then deep-fry the chicken until the meat is cooked and the coating is golden-brown.

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg chicken - cut into pieces
  • 5 pieces calamansi or 1 lemon
  • 4 tbps fish sauce/li>
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsps garlic powder
  • 1/4 cup plain flour
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 liter vegetable or canola oil

Steps:

  1. Marinate the chicken in a mixture of calamansi/lemon, ground black pepper, and 1 tbsp garlic powder for 2 hours in the fridge
  2. Mix the flour, cornstarch, and the rest of the garlic powder. In a separate bowl, beat the eggs.
  3. 20 minutes before you cook the chicken, heat the oil in a deep pan to 350 degrees.
  4. Take the chicken out of the fridge, dip it each chicken pieces in egg, then coat it with the mixture.
  5. Deep-fry for 14 minutes, or until the internal temperature of chicken reaches 165 degrees and the coating is golden brown.
  6. Once the chicken is cooked, put it on a cooling rack to allow the excess oil to drip.
  7. Filipinos eat it with rice and a side of gravy. Enjoy!
